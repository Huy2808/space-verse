Planetary Science

The study of planets, moons, and planetary systems

About Planetary Science

Planetary science is the scientific study of planets (including Earth), moons, and planetary systems, in particular those of the Solar System and the processes that form them. It studies objects ranging in size from micrometeoroids to gas giants, aiming to determine their composition, dynamics, formation, interrelations, and history.

This interdisciplinary field combines aspects of astronomy, atmospheric science, geology, space physics, chemistry, and biology. Planetary scientists work to understand how planetary systems form, how they work, and how all the individual parts interact with each other as a system.

Key Research Areas

Planetary Geology

Study of surface and interior processes of planets and moons, including volcanism, tectonics, impact cratering, and weathering.

Atmospheric Science

Examination of planetary atmospheres, including their composition, structure, dynamics, and evolution over time.

Exoplanet Studies

Investigation of planets orbiting other stars, their potential habitability, and comparison with solar system planets.

Planetary Formation

Research on how planets form from protoplanetary disks, including the processes of accretion, migration, and differentiation.
