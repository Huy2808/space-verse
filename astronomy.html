Astronomy

The study of celestial objects and phenomena beyond Earth's atmosphere

About Astronomy

Astronomy is one of the oldest natural sciences, dating back to antiquity with the observation of celestial bodies. Modern astronomy includes the study of stars, galaxies, exoplanets, the interstellar medium, and the cosmic microwave background. It addresses fundamental questions about the origin and evolution of the universe.

The field combines mathematics, physics, and chemistry to explain the nature of celestial objects and phenomena. Major sub-disciplines include observational astronomy (using telescopes and other instruments to collect data) and theoretical astronomy (developing models to explain observations).

Key Research Areas

Stellar Astronomy

Study of stars, their formation, evolution, and eventual demise. Includes research on stellar classification, nucleosynthesis, and variable stars.

Planetary Science

Examination of planets, moons, and planetary systems, particularly those of the Solar System and the processes that form them.

Galactic Astronomy

Research on the structure and components of our galaxy and others, including the interstellar medium and star clusters.

Cosmology

Study of the universe as a whole, including its origin, evolution, and ultimate fate, through observations and theoretical models.

Quick Facts

  • Oldest science with roots in ancient civilizations
  • Hubble Space Telescope has made over 1.4 million observations since 1990
  • Approximately 100 billion stars in our Milky Way galaxy
  • Neutron stars can rotate up to 700 times per second

Notable Discoveries

Exoplanets

Over 5,000 confirmed planets orbiting other stars, with thousands more candidates awaiting verification.

First confirmed in 1992

Dark Matter

Invisible matter that makes up about 27% of the universe's mass-energy content, detected through gravitational effects.

First proposed in 1933

Gravitational Waves

Ripples in spacetime caused by massive accelerating objects, first directly detected in 2015.

First detected in 2015
