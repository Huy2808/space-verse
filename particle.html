Particle Physics

Exploring the fundamental building blocks of matter and energy

Overview

Particle physics explores the nature of the smallest building blocks of the universe, including quarks, leptons, bosons, and the forces that govern their interactions. It seeks to answer fundamental questions about the origin and structure of matter and energy.

Major research areas include the Standard Model, Higgs boson, neutrino oscillations, dark matter, and high-energy particle accelerators such as the Large Hadron Collider.

Key Research Areas

Standard Model

The theory describing the electromagnetic, weak, and strong nuclear interactions, which mediate the dynamics of known subatomic particles.

High-Energy Physics

Experiments at particle accelerators to probe the properties of fundamental particles at extremely high energies.

Neutrino Physics

Study of neutrinos, their masses, oscillations, and role in the universe.

Dark Matter

Research into the nature and detection of dark matter, which makes up a significant portion of the universe's mass.

Quick Facts

  • The Higgs boson was discovered in 2012
  • Neutrinos rarely interact with matter
  • Particle accelerators reach energies of trillions of electron volts
  • Dark matter has not yet been directly detected
